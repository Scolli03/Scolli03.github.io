My Skills.

Python

Like many developers in recent years, I got my start with Python. What started as making better use of my downtime at my current place of employment ended up saving the company and average of $55,000 a year in time savings alone! This became a pivotal skill that completely altered the course of my career. I started learning Python 10 minutes at a time. Now I get to code all the time!

C# .NET

As my skills began to grow, so did the demand for them. Crazy I know! I was lucky to be employed where the potential for these skills were not underestimated. As more and more departments had ideas of their own as how to increase productivity and reduce cost through custom tools, I was faced with the challenge of how to make sure everyone had the resources necessary to run my programs. I love the tools and capabilities that the .net ecosystem has to offer including the ability to easily distribute my work to anyone in the company. I have also dived headfirst into the new world of .net core and all of its new and exciting features!

SQL

Soon it was not enough to just process data. We needed to store it, generate reports from it, and allow end users to alter some of it without “giving up farm” regarding access and control. When I started programming, we used and Access database file. While it did the job, it posed challenges when it came to leveraging the data within from multiple applications. As the business grew, we eventually upgraded to SQL Server. I knew I had to do everything I could to learn how to leverage what SQL Server has to offer. While there is still even more to learn, I will say that upwards of 90% of our internal applications now leverage the SQL Server.

VBA

Excel is still one of the most used software’s to store and manipulate data. Every business in every industry will likely leverage excel at some level. It is no different where I work. Before my programming days there was so much copying and pasting of data from this excel file to that excel file. Making sure you do not mess up the formatting or formulas when pasting here or there. And there were so many people making manual edits to information that when there was an error it was hard to figure out where and why it arose. Once I got into programming, I decided this skill that was not only a benefit, but essentially a requirement. Not only has it helped us internally via Excel and other VBA integrated software’s such as PolyWorks, but we have also helped develop macros for other international companies!
